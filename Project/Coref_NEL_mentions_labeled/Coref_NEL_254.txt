FACT Philip Tobias (October 1925June 2012) was a South African palaeoanthropologist at the University of Witwatersrand, whose work has contributed enormously to our understanding of how humans evolved. He is best known for his work with fellow palaeoanthropologist Louis Leakey. He identified the ancestral species of human beings known as Homo habilis. His work at the Sterkfontein caves in Gauteng has led to it becoming a World Heritage site. He was also famous for his opposition to apartheid and gave speeches condemning apartheid at protest rallies and also to academic audiences.
